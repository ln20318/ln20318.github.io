The Great Whales of America

Introduction

There are many whales in America. There are big whales. There are small whales. Some whales like to swim in the ocean. Some like to swim in the sea. Some whales don't like swimming at all, but rather swing in the trees. Some whales eat cod and some whales eat oranges. Some whales have baby whales and some live in the city. Whales are a diverse bunch, but we are going to do our best to tackle all the kinds of whales in this informative webpage.

Jumpy Whales

Above you can see a picture of a Jumpy Whale. This is a particular classification of whales that enjoy to jump. As you can see, this one above is jumping out of the water. It is unknown by scientists why these whales jump. However, I did some investigative reporting asked my buddy Dave, a jumpy whale, why he enjoys jumping. "Jump life bro," is what he responded.

Hippopotamus Whales

Similarities between hippopotami and whales:

  • Large
  • Swim (H2O)
  • Mean looking
  • Slimy

After intense research, I discovered that hippopotami are indeed a type of whale. As you can see above, the evidence is overwhelming. These types of whale are particularly nefarious, as they live in the swamp and like to swim eerily in the water.

Moose

The moose is the most landfaring whale of North America. With four legs, it is the most precarious whale in the whale kingdom. Onlookers enjoy watching its horns spike through the surrounding land objects, such as trees and plants. The whale moose whale moose is my personal favorite whale.
